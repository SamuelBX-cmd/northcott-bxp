Why this matters
\n
In a market of 77,000 active providers, differentiation cannot come from features. It comes from behaviour.
\n
\n
Four universal principles govern every Northcott interaction — for customers and families, staff, government partners, and donors. The principles are constant. The expression shifts by audience.

\n
Informed by Lou Downe's Good Services framework & Northcott Strategic Direction 2025

Principle 01
\n
You lead,
we support
\n
Choice and control, made real
\n
Every interaction hands the steering wheel back to the person it most affects. Northcott exists to enable — not to decide, manage, or know better.
\n
    \n
  • Start with goals, not service menus
    • \n
  • Offer options rather than prescriptions
    • \n
  • Make it easy to give feedback and change course
    • \n
\n
Good Services: "Requires no prior knowledge" · "Enables users to complete it unaided"

    • \n
  • Warm, consistent handoffs between teams

Principle 01 — You lead, we support
\n
Every initial conversation starts with "What does a good life look like for you?" — not "What services do you need?" Service options are presented as choices, with trade-offs explained plainly.
\n
Customers can change direction without bureaucratic friction. The system bends to the person, not the other way around.
\n
\n
\n
Principle 02 — Known, not just served
\n
Moving between Northcott services — housing, community, clinical — feels seamless. The new team already knows the person. No one ever has to repeat their story.
\n
Check-ins happen before situations become problems. Milestone moments are noticed and celebrated.
\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n
Principle 03 — Confidence in complexity
\n
    \n
  • The NDIS journey feels less overwhelming with Northcott as a guide
    • \n
  • Information is in plain language — no jargon, no assumptions
    • \n
  • Content helps families understand their options before they even ask
    • \n
  • Every decision is explained — not just enacted
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
Principle 04 — Part of something bigger
\n
    \n
  • People feel connected to a community, not just receiving a service
    • \n
  • Northcott's advocacy work is visible and felt at every level
    • \n
  • Customers are invited into that story — their lived experience shapes how Northcott advocates for systemic change
    • \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n
Customer experience standard: people leave every Northcott interaction feeling more capable, more connected, and more in control than when they arrived.

What living this looks like
\n
These principles are a standard of behaviour. Not a values statement on a wall — a daily operating code.
\n
\n
One Northcott means every staff member — whether frontline or corporate — understands how their role connects to the same inclusion mission. Brand is behaviour. Behaviour is culture.

\n\n
\n
\n
You lead, we support
\n
    \n
  • Empowered to use professional judgement — not just process-followers
    • \n
  • Work across teams to give customers real choice, even when that means an internal referral
    • \n
  • The customer's goal is the north star, not the service category
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
Known, not just served
\n
    \n
  • Continuity of relationship is a quality standard, not a nice-to-have
    • \n
  • Handoffs between teams are warm and briefed — context travels with the person
    • \n
  • Know the person's history before the conversation, not during it
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
Brand imperatives
\n
    \n
  • Confidence in complexity — staff are invested in continuously, with the knowledge to be genuine experts
    • \n
  • Part of something bigger — pride in the work comes from understanding impact, not just completing tasks
    • \n
  • One Northcott culture is the lived version of Principle 04
    • \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n
What good looks like
\n
    \n
  • Staff feel connected to Northcott's purpose — not just their task list
    • \n
  • Leaders and teams talk openly about values in everyday work
    • \n
  • Stories of great teamwork and customer impact are shared and celebrated
    • \n
  • Training, L&D, and internal comms reinforce all four principles consistently
    • \n
\n
\n
\n
What breaks the experience
\n
    \n
  • Handoffs without context — customers repeat their story
    • \n
  • Service-first thinking that overrides customer goals
    • \n
  • Jargon and process language in customer-facing communication
    • \n
  • Teams operating in isolation rather than as One Northcott
    • \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n
Staff standard: every person at Northcott can articulate how their role connects to the inclusion mission — and acts on it.

The strategic relationship
\n
Northcott can advocate credibly because it is doing the work — not just talking about it.

Government and peak bodies need to trust that Northcott's evidence reflects real customer experience. Relationships are sustained, not transactional — Northcott turns up consistently, not just at contract renewal time.

You lead, we support
\n
Northcott operates as a genuine partner, not a dependency. In co-design, policy submissions, and regional planning, it brings customer voice into the room — and defers to it.

\n
Known, not just served
\n
Relationships with government are sustained across administrations and funding cycles. Northcott is a known quantity — credible, consistent, and evidence-grounded.

Confidence in complexity
\n
Northcott communicates with government in their language — evidence-led, outcome-focused, specific about what works. Northcott is the organisation government calls when it needs to understand what's actually happening in the sector.

\n
Part of something bigger
\n
Advocacy is strategic, not reactive. Northcott leads on inclusion, disability employment, and systemic reform. The Stretch RAP and DEI commitments are visible, not just internal aspirations.

Foundation
\n
The Good Services backbone
\n
Lou Downe's 15 Good Services principles define what makes a service genuinely good — developed for the UK Government Digital Service, adopted widely in disability and public sector contexts. Each of Northcott's four brand experience principles maps to the most diagnostically powerful Good Services criteria for that dimension of experience.
